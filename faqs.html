Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know: WICA FAQs

WICA stands for the World Digital Identity and Currency Authority. We operate as an Adoption Community Group under a recognized global framework (analogous to the W3C).

Our core mandate is to move beyond abstract technical specifications and focus squarely on the implementation and market adoption of secure, privacy-preserving Digital Identity standards—such as Verifiable Credentials (VCs) and Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs)—ensuring they work effectively and consistently worldwide.

While "currency" appears in our name, WICA's primary focus is on Digital Identity. We champion the practical adoption of standards like Verifiable Credentials and Decentralized Identifiers to create a secure, privacy-preserving digital ecosystem.

The Global White Paper 2025 is our foundational document that outlines WICA's mandate, governance, and strategic vision. It establishes the framework for public-private collaboration.

The Public-Private Partnership refers to WICA's collaborative approach, bringing together governments, enterprises, and developers to accelerate the adoption of digital identity standards worldwide.

Developers and organizations that join WICA gain access to a global network of experts, implementation resources, and collaboration opportunities. You'll be at the forefront of shaping how digital identity standards are adopted worldwide.

Benefits include early access to technical guidance, participation in working groups, and the ability to influence the practical implementation of emerging standards.
